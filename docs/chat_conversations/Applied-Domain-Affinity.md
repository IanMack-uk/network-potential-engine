# Applied Domain: Affinity

Affinity provides the first empirical domain for the Network Potential framework and serves as an initial test environment for relational potential theory.

The Affinity platform is intended to support individuals in developing and maintaining relational networks by applying the mathematical principles developed in the Network Potential Series. The system is designed to analyse weighted relational networks, estimate structural opportunity, and provide guidance for the formation and development of valuable relationships.

The initial empirical domain consists of small, well-defined student cohorts in creative disciplines, allowing relational structures to be observed with sufficient completeness and stability to support quantitative modelling.

The first planned implementations include:

- approximately 150 students studying Music Business at a single London university, typically divided into:

  - approximately 60 first-year students,

  - approximately 50 second-year students,

  - approximately 40 third-year students,

- a comparable cohort of approximately 150 students studying Art and Design at a second London university.

These cohorts provide controlled relational environments in which:

- potential ties are well-defined,

- interaction frequencies can be observed,

- tie strengths can be estimated,

- network evolution can be tracked over time.

The scale of these cohorts is large enough to exhibit meaningful structural complexity while remaining small enough to permit detailed empirical modelling.

Affinity will provide a practical setting in which:

- relational potentials may be estimated from observed data,

- structural opportunity measures can be computed,

- propagation operators can be approximated,

- equilibrium structures can be analysed,

- theoretical predictions can be compared with observed network evolution.

The Affinity domain therefore serves as a bridge between the theoretical development of relational potential theory and its empirical validation.

While the initial domain is restricted to student cohorts in the creative industries, the underlying framework is intended to generalise to broader relational systems including professional networks and collaborative communities.

Affinity therefore functions as the first empirical implementation of the Network Potential framework and provides a concrete setting in which the mathematical theory can be tested and refined.
